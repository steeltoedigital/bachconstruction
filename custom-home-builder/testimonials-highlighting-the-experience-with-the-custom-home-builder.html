A Vibrant Community with Deep Roots

Marietta, GA is a city rich in history, dating back to its founding in 1834. The city played a significant role during the Civil War, and its storied past is preserved in numerous museums, historic homes, and landmarks. The Marietta Square, the heart of the city, is encircled by historic buildings, boutique shops, and a variety of restaurants, offering a charming and welcoming atmosphere.

The community in Marietta, GA is known for its deep sense of belonging. Residents enjoy a range of events and festivals throughout the year, including the annual Marietta StreetFest, Taste of Marietta, and the Glover Park Concert Series. These events foster a sense of belonging and provide opportunities for residents to connect with one another.

Outstanding Educational Resources

For families considering a move to Marietta, GA, the quality of education is often a top priority. Marietta is served by both the Marietta City Schools district and the Cobb County Schools, both of which are well-regarded in the state. Marietta High School, for example, is known for its comprehensive International Baccalaureate (IB) program, offering students a demanding and globally recognized curriculum.

In addition to public schools, the Marietta area is home to several prestigious private schools, such as Walker School and Mount Paran School, which provide alternative education options for families. For higher education, Kennesaw State University, located nearby, offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, making Marietta an excellent choice for lifelong learners.

A Strong and Diverse Economy

Marietta, GA boasts a thriving and diverse economy, which is a significant factor for those considering relocation. The city benefits from its proximity to Atlanta, providing residents with access to a broad range of job opportunities in various industries, including healthcare, education, tech industries, and manufacturing.

Lockheed, one of the largest employers in the area, has a significant presence in the Marietta region, contributing to the city’s economic stability.

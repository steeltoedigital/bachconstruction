A Dynamic Community Steeped in History

Marietta, Georgia is a city rich in history, dating back to its founding in 1834. The city played a pivotal role during the Civil War, and its historical significance is preserved in numerous museums, historic homes, and landmarks. The historic Marietta Square, the heart of the city, is encircled by historic buildings, boutique shops, and a variety of restaurants, offering a charming and welcoming atmosphere.

The community in Marietta, GA is known for its strong sense of togetherness. Residents enjoy a range of events and festivals throughout the year, including the Marietta StreetFest, the Taste of Marietta event, and the Glover Park Music Series. These events foster a sense of belonging and provide opportunities for residents to connect with one another.

Top-Quality Education Options

For families considering a move to Marietta, Georgia, the quality of education is often a top priority. Marietta is served by both the Marietta City Schools district and the Cobb County School District, both of which are respected in the state. The local high school, for example, is known for its robust International Baccalaureate (IB) program, offering students a challenging and globally recognized curriculum.

In addition to public schools, Marietta is home to several prestigious private schools, such as The Walker School and Mount Paran School, which provide alternative education options for families. For higher education, Kennesaw State University, located nearby, offers a wide range of bachelor's and master's programs, making Marietta an excellent choice for lifelong learners.

A Strong and Diverse Economy

Marietta, GA boasts a thriving and diverse economy, which is a significant factor for those considering relocation. The city benefits from its proximity to Atlanta, providing residents with access to a broad range of job opportunities in various industries, including healthcare, education, technology, and manufacturing.

Lockheed, one of the largest employers in the area, has a significant presence in Marietta, contributing to the city’s economic stability. Additionally, the WellStar Health System, another major employer, offers a range of healthcare services and job opportunities. The city's thriving economy is complemented by a low unemployment rate, making Marietta an attractive destination for professionals seeking career growth.

One of the most compelling reasons to consider moving to Marietta, GA is the affordability of living compared to other parts of the Atlanta metropolitan area. This area offers a range of housing options, from charming historic homes to modern, newly constructed residences. Whether you're a first-time homebuyer or looking for a luxury estate, Marietta has something to offer.

Strategic Location and Accessible Transportation

Marietta, Georgia offers the perfect balance of suburban tranquility and city amenities. Located just 20 miles northwest of downtown Atlanta, Marietta provides easy access to the city's amenities while maintaining a quieter, more relaxed atmosphere. The city's proximity to major highways, including I-75 and I-285, ensures that residents can easily commute to Atlanta for work or leisure.

Public transportation is also readily available, with CobbLinc buses providing connections to the MARTA transit system in Atlanta. For those who travel frequently, Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, one of the busiest airports in the world, is just a short drive away. This area's strategic location and excellent transportation options make it an ideal choice for those who want the best of both worlds.

A Secure and Family-Oriented Community

Safety ranks high for many people when choosing a new place to live, and Marietta, GA excels in this area. The Marietta Police Department is highly regarded for its commitment to community policing and public safety initiatives.

In addition to safety, Marietta offers a variety of family-friendly amenities, including parks, recreational facilities, and community centers. The city is home to the popular Six Flags White Water, a water park that provides endless entertainment for families during the summer months. The Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area, located nearby, offers opportunities for hiking, fishing, and enjoying the natural beauty of the area.

Vibrant Cultural and Outdoor Activities

Marietta, Georgia is a city that values culture and the arts. The Strand Theatre, located on Marietta Square, is a beautifully restored 1930s movie house that now hosts a variety of performances, including concerts, plays, and classic film screenings. The local history museum and the Gone with the Wind Museum are also popular cultural attractions that celebrate the city’s rich heritage.

For those who enjoy outdoor activities, Marietta offers plenty of options.

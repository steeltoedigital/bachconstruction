A Vibrant Community with Deep Roots

Marietta, GA is a city steeped in history, dating back to its founding in 1834. The city played a significant role during the Civil War, and its historical significance is preserved in numerous museums, historic homes, and landmarks. The Marietta Square, the heart of the city, is anchored by historic buildings, boutique shops, and a variety of restaurants, offering a charming and welcoming atmosphere.

The community in Marietta, GA is known for its strong sense of togetherness. Residents enjoy a range of events and festivals throughout the year, including the Marietta StreetFest, the Taste of Marietta event, and the Glover Park Music Series. These events encourage community engagement and provide opportunities for residents to connect with one another.

Outstanding Educational Resources

For families considering a move to Marietta, Georgia, the quality of education is often a top priority. Marietta is served by both the Marietta City Schools district and the Cobb County Schools, both of which are respected in the state. Marietta High School, for example, is known for its comprehensive International Baccalaureate (IB) program, offering students a challenging and globally recognized curriculum.

In addition to public schools, Marietta is home to several prestigious private schools, such as The Walker School and Mount Paran Christian School, which provide alternative education options for families. For higher education, Kennesaw State University, located nearby, offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, making the Marietta area an excellent choice for lifelong learners.

A Thriving and Diverse Economic Environment

Marietta, GA boasts a robust and varied economy, which is a significant factor for those considering relocation. The city benefits from its proximity to Atlanta, providing residents with access to a broad range of job opportunities in various industries, including healthcare, education, tech industries, and industrial production.

Lockheed, one of the largest employers in the area, has a significant presence in Marietta, GA, contributing to the city’s economic stability. Additionally, the WellStar Health System, another major employer, offers a range of healthcare services and job opportunities. The city's thriving economy is complemented by a low unemployment rate, making Marietta, GA an attractive destination for professionals seeking career growth.

Proximity to Atlanta and Transportation

Marietta, Georgia offers the perfect balance of peaceful suburban life and city amenities. Public transportation is also readily available, with CobbLinc buses providing connections to the MARTA transit system in Atlanta. Cultural Richness and Recreational Opportunities

Marietta, Georgia is a city that values culture and the arts. The Strand Theatre, located on the Marietta Square, is a beautifully restored 1930s movie house that now hosts a variety of performances, including concerts, plays, and classic film screenings.
